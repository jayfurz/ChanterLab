# Byzantine Chant Scales — Reference Document

All intervals are given in **moria (μόρια)**, the intervallic units of Byzantine theory. The octave = **72 moria**. Each scale is expressed as a sequence of ascending step sizes between consecutive degrees.

-----

## 1. Degree Names (Martyria / Parallagē)

The seven ascending degrees of the Byzantine scale, with their martyria syllables:

|Index|Greek|Roman|
|-----|-----|-----|
|1    |Νη   |Ni   |
|2    |Πα   |Pa   |
|3    |Βου  |Vou  |
|4    |Γα   |Ga   |
|5    |Δι   |Di   |
|6    |Κε   |Ke   |
|7    |Ζω   |Zo   |
|8    |Νη′  |Ni′  |

These are *positional* degree names within the scale, not fixed pitches.

-----

## 2. Accidentals (Fixed Alterations)

Byzantine notation uses two families of accidentals, each in graded strengths:

- **Sharp (δίεσις / diesis)** — raises a pitch. Graded: `+2`, `+4`, `+6`, `+8` moria.
- **Flat (ὕφεσις / hyphesis)** — lowers a pitch. Graded: `−2`, `−4`, `−6`, `−8` moria.

The most common alterations in chant are the `±4` and `±6` varieties.

-----

## 3. The Four Principal Scales

### 3.1 Octave Natural Diatonic Scale

The foundational diatonic scale, used in Modes I, IV, Plagal I, and Plagal IV (and others in diatonic shading).

|From → To|Moria|
|---------|-----|
|Ni → Pa  |12   |
|Pa → Vou |10   |
|Vou → Ga |8    |
|Ga → Di  |12   |
|Di → Ke  |12   |
|Ke → Zo  |10   |
|Zo → Ni′ |8    |

- **Step sequence:** `12 · 10 · 8 · 12 · 12 · 10 · 8`
- **Cumulative degrees from Ni:** `0, 12, 22, 30, 42, 54, 64, 72`
- **Tetrachord structure:** two conjunct diatonic tetrachords `10–8–12` connected by a whole tone of `12`.

### 3.2 Hard Chromatic Scale

Used for Mode Plagal II (and the hard form of Mode II). Characterized by a narrow semitone and an augmented second.

|From → To|Moria|
|---------|-----|
|Pa → Vou |6    |
|Vou → Ga |20   |
|Ga → Di  |4    |
|Di → Ke  |12   |
|Ke → Zo  |6    |
|Zo → Ni′ |20   |
|Ni′ → Pa′|4    |

- **Step sequence (rooted on Pa):** `6 · 20 · 4 · 12 · 6 · 20 · 4`
- **Cumulative degrees from Pa:** `0, 6, 26, 30, 42, 48, 68, 72`
- **Tetrachord structure:** two hard chromatic tetrachords `6–20–4` connected by a whole tone of `12`.

### 3.3 Soft Chromatic Scale

Used for Mode II (soft chromatic form). Characterized by balanced chromatic tetrachords.

|From → To|Moria|
|---------|-----|
|Ni → Pa  |8    |
|Pa → Vou |14   |
|Vou → Ga |8    |
|Ga → Di  |12   |
|Di → Ke  |8    |
|Ke → Zo  |14   |
|Zo → Ni′ |8    |

- **Step sequence:** `8 · 14 · 8 · 12 · 8 · 14 · 8`
- **Cumulative degrees from Ni:** `0, 8, 22, 30, 42, 50, 64, 72`
- **Tetrachord structure:** two soft chromatic tetrachords `8–14–8` connected by a whole tone of `12`.

### 3.4 Scale of the Grave Diatonic Mode

The diatonic form of Mode Barys (Grave / Plagal III). Rooted functionally on Ga.

|From → To|Moria|
|---------|-----|
|Ni → Pa  |6    |
|Pa → Vou |16   |
|Vou → Ga |8    |
|Ga → Di  |12   |
|Di → Ke  |10   |
|Ke → Zo  |12   |
|Zo → Ni′ |8    |

- **Step sequence:** `6 · 16 · 8 · 12 · 10 · 12 · 8`
- **Cumulative degrees from Ni:** `0, 6, 22, 30, 42, 52, 64, 72`

## 4. Scales with Disjunct Tetrachords (Enharmonic Family)

The enharmonic scales are built from **disjunct tetrachords separated by a whole tone** rather than from conjunct tetrachords. They are characteristic of the Grave Enharmonic Mode (Mode Barys enharmonic).

### 4.1 Grave Enharmonic Mode — from Ζω (Zo)

Enharmonic Barys rooted on low Zo (the Zo below Ni).

|From → To|Moria|
|---------|-----|
|Zo → Ni  |6    |
|Ni → Pa  |12   |
|Pa → Vou |12   |
|Vou → Ga |12   |
|Ga → Di  |6    |
|Di → Ke  |12   |
|Ke → Zo′ |12   |

- **Step sequence:** `6 · 12 · 12 · 12 · 6 · 12 · 12`
- **Cumulative degrees from Zo:** `0, 6, 18, 30, 42, 48, 60, 72`
- **Tetrachord structure:** two enharmonic tetrachords `12–12–6` separated by the disjunctive tone; all non-leading intervals are whole tones of 12, with a characteristic narrow step of 6 at the tetrachord boundary.

### 4.2 Grave Enharmonic Mode — from Γα (Ga)

Enharmonic Barys rooted on Ga, showing the extended tetrachord pattern.

- **Step sequence:** `6 · 12 · 12 · 6 · 12 · 12 · 6 · 12 · 12`
- **Tetrachord generator:** `6–12–12` (a 30-moria tetrachord), chained as stacked units.
- This layout demonstrates the underlying enharmonic tetrachord structure rather than a closed octave; when closed to an octave the final segment adjusts so the total = 72.

## 5. Tetrachord Shadings (χρόαι / Chroai)

Shadings are local alterations applied to a tetrachord within a scale, most often in the diatonic family, to produce expressive color characteristic of specific modes and phrases.

### 5.1 Even (Zygos) on Ga

|Step|Moria|
|----|-----|
|1   |18   |
|2   |4    |
|3   |16   |
|4   |4    |

- **Step sequence:** `18 · 4 · 16 · 4`
- Applied locally on Ga; produces the Zygos character.

### 5.2 Inflected (Kliton) on Ga

|Step|Moria|
|----|-----|
|1   |20   |
|2   |4    |
|3   |4    |
|4   |14   |

- **Step sequence:** `20 · 4 · 4 · 14`
- Applied locally on Ga.

### 5.3 Broad (Spathi) on Ga

Spathi has two commonly cited forms:

**Form A:**

|Step|Moria|
|----|-----|
|1   |14   |
|2   |12   |
|3   |4    |

- **Step sequence:** `14 · 12 · 4` *(plus the closing interval of the containing scale)*

**Form B:**

|Step|Moria|
|----|-----|
|1   |14   |
|2   |4    |
|3   |4    |
|4   |20   |

- **Step sequence:** `14 · 4 · 4 · 20`

## 6. Modulants (Φθοραί / Fthores)

Fthores are signs that signal a **modulation** — a change from the current scale/genus to another — at a specific point in the melodic line. They operate by reassigning the current degree to act as a different degree of a new scale (effectively "pivoting" the tonal center).

The fthores are grouped into three families:

### 6.1 Diatonic Family

Fthores that modulate into (or within) the diatonic genus. Standard fthores exist for each diatonic degree: **Ni, Pa, Vou, Ga, Di, Ke, Zo**.

Effect: redefine the current pitch as the named diatonic degree of a new diatonic scale.

### 6.2 Chromatic Family

Fthores that modulate into the chromatic genus (soft or hard). Principal chromatic fthores operate on **Di** (soft chromatic) and **Pa** (hard chromatic), among others.

Effect: redefine the current pitch as the base of a chromatic tetrachord, triggering soft or hard chromatic intervals in the following melody.

### 6.3 Enharmonic Family

Fthores that modulate into the enharmonic genus. Principal enharmonic fthores operate on **Ga** and **Zo**.

Effect: redefine the current pitch as the base of an enharmonic tetrachord, invoking the `6–12–12` (or equivalent) structure.

## 7. Timing Elements Related to Scale Performance

Two timing signs that affect how scale degrees are sung (not interval content, but relevant to authentic rendering):

### 7.1 Dotted Gorgon (γοργόν with dot)

A subdivision of a beat into unequal parts:

- **Dot before the gorgon:** first portion = `2/3` of the beat, second portion = `1/3`.
- **Dot after the gorgon:** first portion = `1/3` of the beat, second portion = `2/3`.

### 7.2 Upbeat / Downbeat Markers

Two marks appear in the notation system to indicate metric position:

- **Downbeat marker** — indicates the strong/accented beat position.
- **Upbeat marker** — indicates the weak/unaccented beat position.

These govern metric placement and accentuation, not interval content.
