# ChanterLab — Business Model & Market Research (DRAFT for owner discussion)

*2026-07-04 · prepared for GitHub issue #68 · decisions are the owner's; this memo frames them. Every external number is cited inline.*

---

## 1. Thesis

ChanterLab today is a working, mobile-first, no-account choir/chant practice app (live at `chanterlab.com/training/`): pick a piece from a 3,296-piece library, pick your voice, the app plays the *other* parts while you sing yours, with a live pitch trace and per-note scoring. Almost every incumbent in the rehearsal-track market sells a **fixed, pre-made catalog** — you can only practice what the vendor produced. The two products that *do* let a user bring their own score each miss half of what ChanterLab does: **Soundslice** has a PDF/photo OMR scanner and per-voice mute but never grades the singer's voice; **My Choral Coach** grades the singer but needs the director to supply MusicXML or a recording, not a raw PDF. ChanterLab's strategic asset is a deterministic vector-OMR pipeline that just turned 3,296 born-digital liturgical PDFs into per-voice MusicXML at ~99.8% mean integrity — the basis for combining **automatic PDF ingestion + per-voice practice + live mic scoring** in one purpose-built choir tool, which no single competitor does. The recommendation of this memo: **treat the Antiochian Orthodox catalog as a beachhead (not the commercial product), lead the general market with that combined capability, and default all user uploads to private personal-practice use** to keep the rights burden on the uploader, following the Soundslice precedent. The niche is real but small (~2,000 Orthodox parishes); the general choir market it opens onto is ~200,000 US church choirs and ~54 million American singers.

**Reading of the table.** Three clusters. (1) **Fixed-catalog rehearsal tracks** (Cyberbass, ChoraLine, John Fletcher Music, Choir Player, RehearsalMix, Choralia) — good per-voice practice, zero ingestion; you can only practice their catalog. (2) **Ingestion-capable** (Soundslice, MuseScore, Newzik) — Soundslice is the standout: it has a real **PDF/photo OMR scanner**, per-voice solo/mute, and rehearsal-MP3 export, so it *can* run a "PDF → per-voice practice track" workflow one piece at a time — but it never grades the singer's voice, its OMR is a general scan-page-quota'd scanner rather than a deterministic high-integrity liturgical pipeline, and it is a generic music tool, not a choir-practice product. (3) **Grading-capable** (My Choral Coach, SingSharp/mic-trainers) — My Choral Coach is the single closest competitor: it has both **director upload and mic grading**, but the director must supply MusicXML or a reference recording, not a raw PDF. **The intersection none of them close is ChanterLab's: automatic PDF ingestion + per-voice practice + live mic scoring, purpose-built for choirs.** The honest differentiation is not "nobody ingests" (Soundslice does) — it is "nobody ingests *and* grades *and* is built for a choir," backed by a deterministic bulk pipeline that just processed 3,296 pieces with no per-page scan quota.

*Also noted (same clusters):* **Carus music** ("the Choir Coach," [Carus-Verlag](https://www.carus-verlag.com/en/practice-aids-v/practice-aids-for-choirs/carus-music-the-choir-coach/)) — buy-per-voice-part rehearsal app locked to Carus's own editions (publisher storefront, no ingestion, no scoring; exact per-work price not verifiable); **Singerhood** — SATB app with real sung voices (fixed catalog, price undisclosed). Two named products changed hands: RehearsalMix's standalone domain now redirects to MultiTracks, and mychoralcoach.com now resolves to MatchMySound.

## 3. Segments, sizing, and what each pays today

### 3a. Orthodox parishes — the beachhead (existing catalog + owner's network)
- **~2,014 Eastern (Byzantine) Orthodox parishes** in the US, plus 895 Oriental Orthodox = **~2,909 total Orthodox parishes**; Eastern Orthodox parishes report **675,765 adherents / 183,020 regular weekly attendees** ([US Religion Census 2020 / Krindatch, Appendix F](https://www.usreligioncensus.org/sites/default/files/2023-03/Appendix_F--Orthodox_Christian_Groups.pdf)).
- By jurisdiction: **GOARCH ~524 parishes** (375,972 adherents) ([report](https://orthodoxreality.org/wp-content/uploads/2021/09/Report-GOA-From2010To2020Reduced1-1.pdf)); **OCA ~522 parishes** (74,415 adherents) ([report](https://orthodoxreality.org/wp-content/uploads/2022/07/Report-OCA-From2010To2020rev1.pdf)); **Antiochian 255 parishes** (71,216 adherents) ([src](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Antiochian_Orthodox_Christian_Archdiocese_of_North_America)).
- The beachhead catalog already exists and is *freely published*: the Antiochian **Sacred Music Library** offers **2,500+ liturgical PDF pieces free of charge** with composer attribution ([src](https://www.antiochian.org/sacred-music-library)) — the source ChanterLab's 3,296 pieces were OMR-extracted from, used with permission, attribution shown, source PDFs not redistributed.
- **What they pay today:** essentially nothing for practice tooling — chanters currently learn from free PDFs and, at best, free MIDI. This is a *conversion-from-zero* segment: small in absolute count, but high trust density, an owner network, an existing catalog, and no incumbent. Sizing caveat: **~2,000 parishes is the whole niche** — it validates the product and seeds UGC, but it cannot be the revenue base alone.

### 3b. Church choirs generally — the real market
- **~200,000 of the ~250,000 US choruses are church choirs** — the single largest choral segment ([Chorus America, via Alabama Baptist](https://thealabamabaptist.org/vast-majority-of-nations-choruses-are-church-choirs/)); the 2019 update counts **~270,000 choruses and 54M+ American singers** ([Chorus America 2019](https://worldsingingday.org/chorus-impact-study/)).
- Context: **~373,000 US congregations** (US Religion Census 2020 counts 356,642), ~335,000 of them Protestant/other Christian ([Hartford Institute](https://hirr.hartfordinternational.edu/fast-facts-on-american-religion/)). Note the headwind: congregations with a choir fell from **>50% (1998) to ~40% (2018-19)** ([Baptist News / NCS](https://baptistnews.com/article/interest-in-choral-singing-is-not-declining-in-america-so-why-are-church-choirs-disappearing/)) — a practice tool that lowers the effort of running a choir is *counter-cyclical* to this decline.
- **What they pay today:** the worship-band world already pays subscriptions — **RehearsalMix $14.95/mo** ([src](https://www.multitracks.com/products/rehearsalmix/)); classical church choirs buy **per-work tracks at ~$18/work/part (ChoraLine)** ([src](https://www.choraline.com/store/choraline-rehearsal-cds-and-mp3-files)); community-choir members pay **£3/mo (Choir Player)** ([src](https://www.choirplayer.com/subscription-cost/)). Willingness-to-pay for rehearsal tracks is *established*; the ceiling is set by these anchors and the free floor is Cyberbass/Choralia.

### 3c. Community & school choirs — the expansion market
- **~12,000 community/professional choruses and ~38,000 school choruses** (Chorus America type breakdown, [src](https://thealabamabaptist.org/vast-majority-of-nations-choruses-are-church-choirs/)); **~22,000 choral directors** belong to ACDA, "representing over a million singers" ([src](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/American_Choral_Directors_Association)).
- **What they pay today:** community-choir singers pay **$50–$130 per season in dues** (e.g. [Hilton Head Choral Society $130/yr](https://hiltonheadchoralsociety.org/join-us/singer-memberships/), [Greenville $125/semester](https://www.greenvillechoralsociety.com/dues.html)); schools have music-education budgets but tight discretionary spend. This segment is director-led and standards-sensitive (they'll ask about copyright and MusicXML fidelity) — a later expansion, reached through ACDA channels, not the beachhead.

## 4. The moat: OMR ingestion — with honest caveats

**The argument.** No competitor turns an ordinary PDF into *graded* per-voice practice parts (Soundslice gets closest — PDF OMR + per-voice mute + MP3 export — but never grades the singer). The fixed-catalog vendors would each have to *license and produce* every work; ChanterLab can *ingest* it. That inverts the cost structure of the whole category: incumbents grow by buying catalog, ChanterLab grows by letting users bring catalog. The ingestion just ran at scale — 3,296 pieces, ~99.8% mean integrity, no per-page scan quota — which is not a slide, it's a shipped pipeline.

**Caveats the owner should not gloss over:**
1. **Born-digital only, today.** The 99.8% integrity is on *born-digital vector PDFs* (the Antiochian library is typeset digitally). Independent OMR benchmarks put clean digital input at **99%+** but **scanned/photographed scores at only 76–91%**, falling with complexity ([Audiveris](https://audiveris.com/how-accurate-is-audiveris-music-recognition/), [Scoring Notes review](https://www.scoringnotes.com/reviews/a-review-of-optical-music-recognition-software/)). Many real choirs' repertoire is *photocopies and scans* — those need a raster-OMR fallback path that does not exist yet and will never hit 99.8%.
2. **The correction UI is not built.** Per the current roadmap, the per-piece OMR correction/review UI is milestone **M4** — planned, not shipped ([docs/AGILE-RESET-2026-07.md](AGILE-RESET-2026-07.md)). Without it, a user whose upload extracts imperfectly has no in-app way to fix it. For born-digital PDFs this is rare; for scans it is the whole game.
3. **"Nobody does ingestion" is false — say it correctly.** **Soundslice already has a PDF/photo OMR scanner** (JPG/PNG/PDF/HEIC/TIFF → editable MusicXML) plus per-voice solo/mute and rehearsal-MP3 export ([scanner](https://www.soundslice.com/sheet-music-scanner/), [choir practice](https://www.soundslice.com/practice-choir/)) — a choir *could* run "PDF → per-voice track" there today, one piece at a time, gated by a scan-page quota. **My Choral Coach** also uploads + grades, but needs MusicXML/audio ([src](https://matchmysound.com/our-technology/)). So the defensible claim is not "we ingest and they don't"; it is that **no product combines ingestion + per-voice practice + live mic scoring in a choir-purpose UX**, and none demonstrates a *deterministic bulk* pipeline (3,296 pieces, no per-page quota, 99.8% integrity) rather than a general per-page scanner. Test *that* sentence with real directors — and treat Soundslice as the incumbent to out-execute on scoring, choir UX, and bulk throughput, not a market ChanterLab is first to.

**Net:** the moat is a *combination + throughput* advantage, strongest for born-digital PDFs and thin for scans (where Soundslice's general scanner already competes). Sequence the pitch accordingly — lead with "your Finale/Sibelius/MuseScore PDF exports, whole library at once, and it grades your singing," not "photograph your hymnal."

## 5. Rights model for uploads — recommendation and precedent

The Antiochian catalog is **permission-limited** and cannot become the commercial catalog. A user-upload model shifts the rights question to the uploader. This is *not* formal legal advice; it is "how comparable platforms handle it."

**How the precedents structure it:**
- **MuseScore = the cautionary tale.** A public, downloadable UGC score library invited publisher audits: in 2019 Hal Leonard and Disney forced takedowns (even of some public-domain arrangements, by title match); MuseScore's blunt fix was to gate downloads and **auto-flag un-licensed works private**, later adding blanket publisher licenses and a separate licensed "Official Scores" tier ([src](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/MuseScore)). Lesson: *public distribution* is what draws fire.
- **Soundslice = the clean precedent to copy.** It frames itself as *software, not a catalog*: "our customers are responsible for securing the rights to whatever music they're using Soundslice with," the user retains ownership, indemnifies Soundslice, and content defaults to **allow-listed / secret-link (private) access** ([licensing FAQ](https://www.soundslice.com/licensing/faq/), [terms](https://www.soundslice.com/terms/)).
- **Ultimate Guitar = the scale-then-license path** (grow on user transcriptions, later blanket-license via the Harry Fox Agency + majors) ([src](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ultimate_Guitar)) — relevant only if ChanterLab ever wants a *public* shared catalog.
- **DMCA §512(c)** shields hosts that designate a DMCA agent, run a repeat-infringer policy, and do notice-and-takedown; there is **no duty to monitor** ([copyright.gov/512](https://www.copyright.gov/512/)). A musical **arrangement is a derivative work** needing permission — *except* when the underlying work is **public domain**, in which case anyone may arrange it ([copyright.gov circ 14](https://www.copyright.gov/circs/circ14.pdf), [MPA](https://www.mpa.org/creating-authorized-choral-arrangements/)). **This favors ChanterLab's core use case: traditional chant melodies are largely public domain, so exposure is confined to modern published arrangements and modern editions.**

**Options:**
- **(A) Private-by-default, personal-practice only (recommended).** Uploads are visible only to the uploader (and, later, their invited choir). No public sharing tier at launch. Terms put rights responsibility on the uploader (Soundslice model). This narrows the infringement surface to near-nothing because nothing is *publicly distributed* — the reason both Soundslice and post-2019 MuseScore keep un-cleared works private.
- **(B) Private + invited-choir sharing.** Same, extended to a closed roster (director shares with their own singers). Still no public catalog. Slightly more exposure (the copy is shared, not just personal), manageable with the DMCA posture. This is the natural "parish/choir license" shape.
- **(C) Public shared catalog.** Do **not** do this early — it is the MuseScore/UG path and needs blanket licensing before it's safe.

**Recommendation: launch (A), grow into (B) for paid choir licenses, keep (C) off the table until there's a licensing budget.** Add a DMCA agent + takedown page regardless (cheap, and it's the price of admission for hosting user content).

## 6. Pricing candidates (with the anchors found)

Anchors from research: free floor **Cyberbass/Choralia/John Fletcher** ($0 tiers); solo trainers **SingSharp ~$14.99/mo**; community app **Choir Player £3/mo/member**; per-seat catalog **John Fletcher £3.57/seat/yr** (15+ seats); worship **RehearsalMix $14.95/mo (team of 5)**; per-work **ChoraLine ~$18/work/part**; tool subscriptions **Soundslice $50/yr, MuseScore Pro $49.99/yr**; **the most direct comparable — My Choral Coach's choir license $175–$495/yr by choir size**; choir dues **$50–130/season**.

**Structure 1 — Freemium solo + choir license (recommended).**
- **Free solo tier:** the current app — pick from the public/permission-cleared library, practice, singscope, per-note scoring. This is the acquisition funnel and the UGC engine (§7). Beats Cyberbass on UX, matches its price.
- **Choir license: ~$99–$199/yr per choir** (flat, unlimited members) — includes **private repertoire upload** (option B), director dashboard, section assignments. Anchored directly against **My Choral Coach's $175–$495/yr choir tiers** (the one comparable that also does upload + grading) and undercutting "20 members × £3/mo Choir Player ≈ $900/yr," while beating "a few ChoraLine works ≈ $100+." A flat parish price is an easy yes for a music director with a small budget.
- **BYO-repertoire premium** is *inside* the choir license (it's the reason to buy), not a separate SKU.

**Structure 2 — Per-seat SaaS (director-led segments).**
- **$3–$5/member/mo**, director-managed roster, mirrors Choir Player (£3) and RehearsalMix's per-user logic. Better for large ACDA-style choirs; higher friction for small parishes. Offer as an alternative to the flat choir license for 30+ member groups.

**Structure 3 — Free + individual Pro (prosumer).**
- **Individual Pro ~$4/mo or ~$40/yr** (unlock upload-your-own-PDF for personal practice, advanced scoring/strictness, offline). Priced right at the Soundslice ($50/yr) / MuseScore Pro ($49.99/yr) tool-subscription band. Captures the solo chanter or the one choir member who wants their own part, without needing the whole choir to buy in.

**Suggested blend:** Free solo (always) → **Individual Pro ~$40/yr** (Structure 3) as the low-friction upgrade → **Choir license ~$149/yr flat** (Structure 1) as the main revenue line, with per-seat (Structure 2) offered to big choirs. Keep the Orthodox beachhead **free** — it's marketing and content supply, not a revenue center.

## 7. 90-day go-to-market sketch (ties to the in-app recording feature, #67)

The sprint is already building **in-app practice recording** (mic + accompaniment mixed) — that feature *is* the growth engine, because the highest-leverage motion for a singing app is making the practice output shareable. Precedent: Smule grew to ~50M MAU on shared performance clips (Jessie J's "Flashlight" drew 1M+ user duets) ([src](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Smule)); Simply Sing cut acquisition cost ~39% and lifted conversions ~75% with TikTok creator content ([TikTok case study](https://ads.tiktok.com/business/en-US/inspiration/simplysing-scale-with-tiktok)); the GuitarTuna/Yousician wedge (free utility → 100M+ downloads → paid learning) ([src](https://guitartuna.com/about)) is the niche-to-general template.

- **Days 0–30 — Orthodox beachhead + UGC seed.** Ship recording (#67). Recruit 10–20 chanters/choirs from the owner's network; instrument a one-tap "share my practice clip" (Reels/TikTok/Shorts) that watermarks `chanterlab.com`. Every shared clip is both product and free ad. Reach out to the **Antiochian Dept. of Sacred Music** and its Sacred Music Institute for an endorsement/mention ([src](https://www.antiochian.org/sacred-music-library)).
- **Days 30–60 — Prove ingestion for choir directors.** Turn on **private PDF upload** (born-digital first) for 3–5 friendly directors; measure extraction integrity and time-to-first-practice-part. Case-study the best result. Expand Orthodox outreach to **PSALM** (pan-Orthodox, ~170-attendee conference) and **NFGOCM** (Greek) — trust-based, endorsement-driven channels ([src](https://www.orthodox-psalm.org/)).
- **Days 60–90 — Cross the niche line.** Publish 2–3 UGC-driven clips into general choir/worship hashtags; pilot the **choir license** with one non-Orthodox church choir using its own PDF exports. Prepare an **ACDA** touch (22,000 directors, Exhibits Fair, *Choral Journal*) — the standard adoption path for choral software ([src](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/American_Choral_Directors_Association)). Success metric: ≥1 paying choir + a repeatable "upload → parts → shared clip" loop.

Guardrails from the precedents: respect the incumbent notation ecosystem (interoperate with MusicXML/MuseScore, the EZ Byzantine fonts, Neanes) — in the Orthodox niche, free/open positioning and standards-compatibility are near-prerequisites before any paid ask.

## 8. Open decisions for the owner

1. **Beachhead price = free?** Recommend yes (marketing + content supply). Confirm the Antiochian permission covers a *practice tool built on* the PDFs (attribution shown, PDFs not redistributed) — is that already in writing?
2. **Which pricing blend** — flat choir license (~$149/yr), per-seat (~$4/mo), or individual Pro (~$40/yr) as the *primary* SKU? (Memo leans flat choir license as the main line, individual Pro as the funnel.)
3. **Upload scope at launch:** private-personal-only (A) or private + invited-choir (B)? (Recommend A now, B with the paid choir license.)
4. **Scanned-score fallback:** invest in raster OMR + the M4 correction UI now (widens the market to photocopied repertoire) or stay born-digital-only and pitch "your PDF exports" until traction proves demand?
5. **DMCA posture:** stand up a DMCA agent + takedown page before opening uploads? (Recommend yes — low cost, table stakes.)
6. **How generic to go, how fast:** stay Orthodox+liturgical for 6–12 months, or push general church/worship immediately? (Beachhead is small — ~2,000 parishes — so the general market is where revenue lives, but the niche is where trust and UGC start.)
7. **Public sharing tier:** commit to *never* (private-only forever) or reserve it behind future blanket licensing? (Recommend defer indefinitely.)
